Values

Here at Te Peka Sounds, we believe it is important to stay in communcation with the local iwi. Working together ensuring that we continue to take good care of our local district of Te Peka Sounds. In the Maori language there are a variety of moral values that we believe in. By promoting and practising these values, it will help us to look after one another and the environment that we live in. The values are the three P's - partnership, protection and participation.

1 Partnership: Getting along with various community groups, working with the local iwi, to bring the community together to discuss what is important to our environment simultaneously involving the Maori culture strongly.

2 Protection: Upon identifiying the threats and concerns, we can then develop strategies of different tasks to help eliminate them altogether.

3 Participation: Once we have a plan then events can be organised to make it easer for people to report any inappropriate actions that they have spotted. Encouraging the community to take ownership not only in keeping the environment clean and friendly but also actively donating their time and financial resouces.

  • Kotahitanga-Oneness
  • Mohiotanga-Sharing of information
  • Kaitiakitanga-Guard our treasure
  • Wairua-Sense of well-being
  • Tikanga-Obeying the rules
  • Mauri-Beauty of life
  • Manakitanga-Helping each other
  • Whanaungtanga-Sense of belonging
  • Maramatanga-Understanding

Population Growing!

As you are reading this article, the population living on the coast of the Te Peka Sounds is growing. Besides this growing population, many others from different parts of the world appreciates the magnificent view as they came to explore and enjoy the beautiful beaches of Te Peka Sounds. This is where we need your expertise in suggesting what activities and infrastructure is crucial to create an everlasting memory for all who are coming to experience Te Peka Sounds and not forgetting you, our highly valued locals who can enjoy Te Peka Sounds any time they wish!

Preserve Habitat!

Early this year 2018, Friends of Te Peka Sounds celebrated its first birthday. To increase our presence, we invited Jim Wiles the founder of Bird Habitat Society who delivered an outstanding presentation giving us invaluable world of information of ways to help preserve the endangered species and migratory species that is found in Te Peka Sounds. With the fast pace technology ever improving, we are confident we will be able to achieve our goals in preserving our heritage at an excellent standard for the many generations to come.
