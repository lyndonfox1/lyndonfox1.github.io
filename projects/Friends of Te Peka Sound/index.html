Friends of Te Peka Sounds is a newly formed society in 2017. It was birth as a result of the community's concern over ideas for extensive reclamation of Te Peka Sounds tidal flats. Being new, the Friends of Tep Peka Sounds have thrived to protect the estuaries, shores and coastal water that are facing serious threats at a rapid speed. Our famous scenic environment with a welcoming climate turns this land into a paradise for recreation and holiday destination. At the same time, note that there are a few well established commercial fishery operations along our coast and in the deep blue sea. The population in Te Peka Sounds is increasing at a speedy rate. This Northern coast at the top has a few massive estuarine areas equivalent to a tropical rain forest in terms on environmental productivity.

Stop the Pollution!

Over 20 species of local and migrating birds are known to take good advantage of these estuaries which is of high importance to some wader species both internally and externally. Such richness of birdlife potrays a healthy strong community of invertebrate and plant life that supports both birds and commercial and recreational fish. The estuaries are utilized by the fish as feeding, breeding nurseries, passage to fresh water and as hibernation area if needed to recover from injuries. It is extremely essential to the productivity and fishing activity at the top of the northern coastal waters.
